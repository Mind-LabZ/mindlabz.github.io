ECO : SYSTEM

A mobile, grid-based strategy game where players build a thriving land under pressure. Each year begins with a world event that reshapes the map, then players spend energy to adapt. The year ends with a readable report: what changed, why it changed, and what breaks next if you ignore it.

One‑Sentence Hook

ECO / SYSTEM is a fast, readable strategy game where your ability to act is something you build — and every upgrade changes the ecosystem in ways you must manage.

Core Loop

  • Start of year: a world event alters the grid (growth, drought, blight, flood, etc.).
  • Player phase: spend energy on contextual actions per tile (build, upgrade, purify, spread water).
  • End of year: a report shows energy production/spend and resource shifts.
  • Win condition: survive and expand through escalating events; avoid collapse thresholds.
Readability
Clear cause → effect feedback
Depth
Trade‑offs, adjacency, events
Retention
Short sessions, “one more year”
Scalability
Easy to add tiles/events

What Makes It Publishable

  • Strong onboarding: Year 1 doubles as a narrative tutorial.
  • High clarity: end‑of‑year report explains resource movement and drivers.
  • Mobile‑native interaction: tap tile → actions appear; no heavy menus.
  • Expandable content: events + tiles are data‑driven; rapid iteration.

Systems (Prototype Scope)

  • Resources: Nature, Crops, Crystals (avoid too low/high), Pollution (must stay below 100).
  • Energy economy: produced by infrastructure each year; energy is your action budget.
  • Tiles: forest, farm, water, industry, solar, empty, blight/depleted states.
  • Events: drought/flood/blight/boom growth that modify tiles at start of year.
